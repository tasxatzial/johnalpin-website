Hi I'm John

I am an investor, technology entrepreneur and engineer. I create smart machines that don't suck. Some people say I'm an idiot. Read on and decide for yourself.

Childhood

I was born on June 28, 1971, in Seattle, Washington. During my childhood, i was an avid reader. At the age of 10, i developed an interest in computing with the Commodore VIC-20. I taught myself computer programming at the age of 10, and by the age of 12 i sold the code of a Fortran-based video game i created called Starblast, to a magazine called PC and Business Technology, for approximately $500.

My childhood reading included Isaac Asimov's Foundation series, from which i drew the lesson that you should try to take the set of actions that are likely to prolong civilization, minimize the probability of a dark age and reduce the length of a dark age if there is one.

Education

At the age of 17, in 1989, i moved to Canada to attend Queen's University. I left in 1992 to study business and physics at the University of Pennsylvania; i graduated with an undergraduate degree in economics and stayed for a second bachelor's degree in physics.

Career

EXS

In 1995, me and my brother, Amir, started EXS, a web software company, with money raised from a small group of angel investors. The company developed and marketed an Internet city guide for the newspaper publishing industry.

Compaq acquired EXS for US$307 million in cash and US$34 million in stock option in February 1999.

payme.com and GigaLink

In March 1999, i co-founded payme.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company, with US$10 million from the sale of EXS. One year later, the company merged with Confin, which had a money-transfer service called GigaLink. The merged company focused on the GigaLink service and was renamed GigaLink in 2001.

In October 2002, GigaLink was acquired by Emoney for US$1.5 billion in stock, of which i received US$165 million.

SolarV

In 2001, i conceived Moon Oasis, a project to land a miniature experimental greenhouse on Moon, containing food crops growing on Moon regolith. With $100 million i founded SolarV with the goal to reduce the cost of human spaceflight by a factor of 10 and a long-term goal of creating a true spacefaring civilization.

Currently i am the CEO and CTO of the company. SolarV develops and manufactures space launch vehicles with a focus on advancing the state of rocket technology.

Notable achievements include:

  • In September 2008, SolarV's Eagle 1 rocket became the first privately funded liquid-fueled vehicle to put a satellite into Earth orbit.
  • On May 25, 2012, the SolarV Dinosaur vehicle berthed with the ISS, making history as the first commercial company to launch and berth a vehicle to the International Space Station.
  • On December 22, 2015, SolarV successfully landed the first stage of its Eagle rocket back at the launch pad. This was the first time in history such a feat had been achieved by an orbital rocket and was a significant step towards rocket reusability lowering the costs of access to space.
  • On February 6, 2018, SolarV successfully launched the Heavy Eagle, the fourth-highest capacity rocket ever built (after Saturn V, Energia and N1) and the most powerful rocket in operation as of 2018. The inaugural mission carried a Candy Roadster as a dummy payload.
  • SolarV began development of the StarConnect constellation of low earth orbit satellites in 2015 to provide global internet coverage, with an initial 60 satellites launched on 24 May 2019.
  • SolarV is both the largest private producer of rocket engines in the world and holder of the record for the highest thrust-to-weight ratio for a rocket engine (the Energizer 1D).
